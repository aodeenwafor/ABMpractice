AGING GRACEFULLY

Those aching muscles, the knee, the waist, oh I haven’t had a bowl movement in days, what was I saying again? I can’t remember”. These are some of the things we may experience as we age. The good news is we can control how we age (to some extent); it all needs a little effort. Genetics also plays a role in the outcome of your health as you age. Medical history and how one lived in their younger years may affect the quality of our life when much older.


Here are some tips on staying healthy as you get older.

Physical Activity
According to Canada physical activity guidelines, it is beneficial to add muscle and bone strengthening activities using major muscle groups at least twice a week. This will help your posture and balance. A few minutes walk around the home, light gardening, strength and flexibility training while seated helps.
Protein Needs
Adequate daily protein is needed to replace losses, but too much protein can be hard on the ailing kidneys of older adults (p.635). Meat, poultry, fish, eggs, legumes such as beans, peas and lentils, tofu, nuts and seeds are excellent sources of protein, iron and B vitamins.
Vitamins are very important
Fruit and vegetables contain lots of vitamins, minerals and fibre. Choose dark green or brightly coloured vegetables and fruit more often. Grab an apple for an afternoon snack instead of cupcake. Spice up your meals with these beautiful foods like adding berries or sliced bananas in your cereal, have vegetables with dinner.
Maintaining Your Weight
As you age having a healthy weight is very important. This can be achieved by making good food choices like cutting down on saturated fats in dairy foods and meats and eating slightly more vegetables and whole-grain.

Are you eating alone? Canada food guide says enjoying healthy foods with family, friends is a great way to connect and add enjoyment to your life. It can provide many benefits and contribute to a healthy lifestyle. Eating alone can result in a sense of isolation and feelings of loneliness, especially at mealtimes which can result in a loss of appetite which can impact your intake of important nutrients.Check out these resources for more information and to connect with other seniors.
